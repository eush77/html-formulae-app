Syntax conventions:

    • indices and powers: “x^2”, “x_2”
    • any correct HTML (if properly escaped), including tags and entities
    • special symbol sequences:
      “mbscriptA” – “mbscriptZ”, “mbscripta” – “mbscriptz”Mathematical bold script letters (𝓐–𝓩, 𝓪–𝔃)
      “setF”, “setC”, “setR”, “setQ”, “setZ”, “setN”, “setP”Abstract and common number sets
      “+”, “-”, “*”, “/”, “&&”, “||”, “!”Arithmetic and logical operators
      “=”, “==”, “<”, “<=”, “>”, “>=”, “!=”, “/=”, “~”, “~~”, “<<”, “>>”Comparison relations
      “<=>”, “=>”, “=<”, “<==>”, “==>”, “==<”, “|-”, “|=”, “TT”, “BB”Inference relations and constants
      “<->”, “->”, “<-”, “<-->”, “-->”, “<--”Arrows
      “]]”, “:=”, “=def=”“Let” and defining signs
      “+-”, “-+”, “&”, “oo”Various symbols
      “---”, “--”Dashes: em-dash and en-dash
      “``”, “''”, “<<<”, “>>>”Quotation marks
      “␣␣␣”, “␣␣”, “.”Whitespace sequences: em-space, en-space, and thin-space (respectively)

    Notes:

    1. “\” is the escape character
    2. “{}”-groupings in indices and powers are available
    3. indices and powers can also be nested: x^{x_0}
    4. newlines are encoded and preserved
    5. hyphens are not interpreted as minuses in compound words
    6. “TT” or “BB” are left as is, if found close to an alphanumeric character
    7. “⇔”, “⇒”, and “⇐” and their long equivalents preserve appropriate spacing on both sides
